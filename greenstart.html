Taming Unemployment
At EnterpriseNGR, we decided to not just point out the problem of unemployment, but to bring a solution to it. That is why we created the Youth of Enterprise (YOE) Internship Programme.
Youth of Enterprise (YOE) Internship Programme is one of EnterpriseNGR’s immediate developmental initiatives that aims to reverse the national youth unemployment and under-employment trends as well as build capacity in the private sector.
The National Population Commission places Nigeria’s population at about 220 million with 50% of the population comprising youths. Unfortunately, over 38.5% of Nigerian youths remain unemployed and a larger percentage are underemployed.
We seek to improve the employment and employability of Nigerian youth within the Financial and Professional Services (“FPS”) sector with the YOE Internship Programme.
We are on a Mission.
Everywhere you look, you are faced with graduates who, after searching tirelessly for jobs, are forced to settle for menial jobs or worse, dovetail into depression due to the financial, emotional, and physical pressure unemployment brings.
Some have passed through a system that did not fully equip them for the demands of the corporate world and hence, experience the shock of being labeled “unemployable” when they eventually get a chance to prove their worth.
But all that is about to change. We are on a mission to equip Nigerian graduates with the requisite skills, qualifications and experience they need to excel in the financial services sector and stand shoulder to shoulder with their global counterparts through the Youth Of Enterprise (YOE) initiative.
Through the YOE Programme, EnterpriseNGR is playing a vital role in facilitating capacity building in the private sector and contributing to resolving the problem of unemployment in Nigeria.
