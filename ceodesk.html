New Report: From the CEO’s Desk
News,Media |
April 12, 2022 |
Obi Ibekwe |
Comments: 0
From the CEO’s Desk

I want to take this opportunity to reflect on the EnterpriseNGR journey since our launch in October 2021. It has been a superb start for us as we embark on our mission to make Nigeria the premier financial services centre in Africa.


Our launch event tagged, the Voices of Enterprise , was a huge success with high-level speakers including amongst others the Vice President of Nigeria, the globally acclaimed investment and finance guru, Bayo Ogunlesi, our friends from Enterprise Singapore, and TheCityUK – two organisations we seek to model. Attendance at the virtual event was phenomenal and the discourse was at peak levels. That was indeed our big bang moment and a great start.


Welcome to Our New Members

Following our official launch, we have focused on strengthening our organisational structures and expanding our membership base. I'm delighted to report that we have increased our membership base by 50% since our official launch. I wish acknowledge our new Founding Members: DLM Capital Group, Investment One Financial Services Limited and Custodian Investment Plc. who by this membership have made a lifelong commitment towards building the Nigerian financial and professional services ecosystem.


I also thank our new Gold subscription members who include the Bank of Industry of Nigeria, First Ally Capital Limited, and Polaris Bank. We are truly encouraged by your support. Recognition also goes out to our new Silver and Bronze members – we appreciate you all.


New Partnerships

In creating a strong internal structure, we have focused on entrenching global best practices by engaging like-minded international organisations. I am pleased to note that we have entered a knowledge-sharing arrangement with TheCityUK, the leading advocacy group in the United Kingdom.


We attended the Kenya Fintech Forum an event organized by the Nairobi International Financial Centre Authority (NIFCA) and TheCityUK. It is the flagship event of the collaborative NIFCA / TheCityUK Fintech Project. We were invited to attend by TheCityUK with a view to explore similar collaborations for Nigeria. The event was eye opening and we are poised to support the Fintech revolution in Nigeria.


TheCityUK also graciously introduced us to the World Alliance of International Financial Centers (WAIFC) and has agreed to support our bid to become an Observer at the organisation as a prelude to seeking full membership.


WAIFC is a non-profit association registered in Brussels, Belgium. It represents leading international financial centers and facilitates cooperation and the exchange of best practices. WAIFC members are city governments, associations, and institutions similar to ours developing and promoting their financial centers. WAIFC presently has 20 Members across all the continents of the world. Member organisations include TheCityUK, Paris Europlace, Qatar Financial Centre, Dubai International Financial Centre Authority, Hong Kong Financial Services Development Council, FinCity.Tokyo, Toronto Finance International, Casablanca Finance City and Rwanda Finance Limited among others.


We attended the WAIFC Panel and observed their Board Meeting held in Kigali, Rwanda. The event presented us with a unique opportunity to network and associate with members of WAIFC who were all pleased that Nigeria is ready to take its rightful place in the committee of nations. WAIFC would like to see the development of more international finance centers on the African continent and are very keen to support us.


These partnerships are aimed at shortening our learning curve while ensuring we start achieving positive impact in no time.


Continuing our Advocacy Work

With respect to our advocacy work we have identified data as a key factor for analysing the issues affecting the sector and the country at large. We had a meeting with the Statistician General of the Federal Republic of Nigeria and we are working on Terms of Reference for a partnership to generate accurate and granulated data for the Financial and Professional Services sector to support. optimal policy formulation.


I am excited to note that we are currently putting finishing touches to our very first research output, the State of Enterprise report which will be officially launched in Q2 of 2022. The report will spotlight the sector and showcase baseline statistics with a view to highlight the importance of the sector and also serve as a basis to measure our performance against agreed indices.


I am also particularly delighted to report on our progress regarding our first direct intervention tagged, the Youth of Enterprise (YOE). This initiative is focused on onboarding 5,000 youth annually with a view to upskill and place them in 6 months paid internships with our partner organisations. The initiative will officially go live in Q2 of 2022, and we remain confident that the impact YOE will have on the Nigerian economy will be far-reaching.


The Future is Bright

Though it is still early days for us at EnterpriseNGR, I dare say, the dream is truly coming to life and we are well on the path to laying a solid foundation towards achieving our lofty goals.


I do hope you enjoy this first instalment of our quarterly newsletter and I enjoin you all to join hands with us to build the FPS sector and Nigeria at large.


Thank you,

Obi Ibekwe
CEO.
